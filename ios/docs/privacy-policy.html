Kaleidescope — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 30, 2026

Kaleidescope ("the app", "we", "us") is a free collection of classic games. This policy explains what we collect, why, and your choices. We keep it short because we collect as little as possible.

What we collect

  • Phone number — used only to sign you in. When you enter your number, our authentication provider sends a one-time code by SMS to verify it's you. We store your number to recognize your account on future sign-ins.
  • Profile — a display name and an avatar (emoji + color) you choose, shown to you and (in future social features) to friends you connect with.
  • Game activity — your scores and progress, stored on your device and in your account so they follow you between sessions, and on leaderboards you choose to play.
  • Advertising data — the app shows a banner ad through Google AdMob, which may collect a device advertising identifier and limited device/usage information to serve and measure ads.

We do not collect your contacts, location, photos, or microphone, and we do not sell your personal information.

Service providers

  • Supabase — stores your account, profile, and scores.
  • Twilio — delivers the SMS verification code.
  • Google AdMob — serves the in-app banner ad (see Google's privacy policy and AdMob partner data use).

Your choices and rights

  • Limit ad tracking — iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking, and → Apple Advertising.
  • Delete your account — contact us and we'll delete your account, profile, and associated data.

Children

Kaleidescope is rated 10+ and intended for a general audience. It is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us data, contact us and we'll remove it.

Data retention

We keep your account data while your account exists. When you delete your account, we delete your profile and scores. Verification codes are short-lived and not retained after sign-in.

Changes

We may update this policy as the app evolves; material changes are reflected by the date above.
